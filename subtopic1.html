Ethical Hacking

Resource 1

URL: It takes a pirate to know one: ethical hackers for healthcare cybersecurity
Author: BMC
Abstract: Ethical hacking involves professionals using their hacking skills to test and secure systems. Unlike malicious hackers, ethical hackers help organizations strengthen their security by identifying vulnerabilities before bad actors can exploit them. This practice has become integral to cybersecurity, especially for businesses that handle sensitive data. IBM's article discusses ethical hacking's importance in cybersecurity, common techniques, and the growing demand for ethical hackers in the industry.

Resource 2

URL: The Evolution of Ethical Hacking: From Curiosity to Cybersecurity
Author: National Cybersecurity Alliance
Abstract: Ethical hacking is the act of testing a system for vulnerabilities by simulating cyberattacks. The goal is to discover flaws that could potentially be exploited by malicious hackers. This article provides a detailed explanation of HOW ethical hacking has evolutionized its role in preventing cyberattacks, the steps involved, and how ethical hackers differ from black-hat hackers. It also covers its growth throughout the years.
